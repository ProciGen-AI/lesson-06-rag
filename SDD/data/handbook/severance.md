# Severance

## Purpose

Severance is meant to give employees a practical landing period when NovaOps ends employment for business reasons or for performance reasons that are not gross misconduct. It is not a substitute for honest performance management, and it is not a promise that every separation will be identical in every country. Local law, employment agreements, and required consultation processes may add rights or process steps.

People Operations owns final severance calculations. Managers should not promise a specific package without HR review.

This policy is written for retrieval and planning clarity, but it is not a private employment contract. If a local agreement, works council process, statutory notice period, or required consultation process gives a different right, NovaOps follows the more specific rule. People Operations will explain which pieces are company policy and which pieces are local legal process when a separation is being planned.

## Eligibility

Employees may be eligible for severance when NovaOps initiates an involuntary separation because of role elimination, restructuring, lack of work, or documented underperformance. Employees are not normally eligible for severance if they resign voluntarily, abandon their job, engage in fraud or serious misconduct, violate confidentiality obligations, or refuse reasonable transition duties during notice.

Contractors are governed by their contract and are not covered by this severance policy unless their written agreement says otherwise.

Employees on approved family, parental, medical, or disability leave are not excluded from severance eligibility. If a role elimination affects someone on leave, People Operations must review timing, communications, benefits, and local obligations before the manager contacts the employee. Managers should not ask for medical details or pressure someone to return early to discuss a role change.

Performance-based severance is available only when the performance process has been documented and People Operations has approved the final separation. A manager who skips coaching, avoids direct feedback, or waits until the last day of a project should not assume severance can clean up a poor process. See the manager playbook for the underperformance process; the employee-facing fact is simpler: NovaOps expects serious concerns to be communicated before they become separation decisions, except in cases of misconduct or severe risk.

## Formula

The base severance amount is 4 weeks of regular base pay. Once an employee has at least one completed year of NovaOps service, NovaOps adds 2 weeks of regular base pay for each completed year of service. Severance is capped at 16 weeks of pay, which is treated as 4 months for policy purposes.

Examples: an employee with less than one completed year receives 4 weeks. An employee with 2 completed years receives 8 weeks: 4 base weeks plus 2 weeks for each completed year. An employee with 3 completed years receives 10 weeks. An employee with 8 completed years would calculate to 20 weeks, but the cap limits the severance payment to 16 weeks.

The formula uses completed years of continuous NovaOps service, measured from the employee's most recent full-time employee start date through the last paid day. Partial years do not round up. If an employee was a contractor and later became an employee, contractor time does not count unless the written conversion agreement says it does. If an employee transferred countries, People Operations will review whether the local employment record treats the service as continuous.

Regular base pay means normal salary or base wages before bonuses, commissions, referral awards, spot recognition, equity, expense reimbursements, or allowances. For Sales employees, severance is not calculated from pipeline value or expected commissions. For employees with a recent approved compensation change, Finance and People Operations decide whether the new rate or prior rate applies based on the effective date.

Common examples: an employee with 1 completed year receives 6 weeks: 4 base weeks plus 2 weeks for one completed year. An employee with 4 completed years receives 12 weeks. An employee with 6 completed years receives 16 weeks because the formula would reach the cap. If local law requires a greater amount, the local legal amount controls.

## Notice, Release, and Timing

Some separations include a notice period, garden leave, or local consultation period before the final employment date. That time is separate from the severance formula unless People Operations says otherwise in writing. Employees should be told three dates clearly: the last working day, the last paid day, and the expected severance payment date.

NovaOps may require a signed separation agreement and release where legal and appropriate. If a release is required, the severance payment will not be made until the release is signed and the revocation period, if any, has expired. If a release is not legally permitted in the employee's location, People Operations will document the alternate process.

Severance is normally paid through payroll, with required tax withholding. It is not reimbursed through expenses and should not be invoiced by the employee. Finance confirms payment timing after People Operations approves the final packet.

## Vacation, Benefits, and Equipment

Unused vacation is paid out at separation where required by local law. As a NovaOps baseline, we also pay out unused vacation from the employee's vacation bank unless a local plan requires a different treatment. Negative vacation balances are not clawed back.

Health coverage, pension, retirement contributions, and statutory benefits follow local plan rules. NovaOps may provide up to 2 months of company-paid health benefit continuation where that structure exists and is legal.

Employees must return NovaOps equipment according to [managing work devices](managing-work-devices.md). IT disables system access through Okta on the agreed schedule.

Unused holidays are not paid out. Learning budgets, wellbeing allowances, home-office allowances, and referral bonuses stop at separation unless a written plan says otherwise. Expenses incurred before the last working day remain reimbursable if they were approved, documented, and submitted within 30 days.

System access removal is coordinated through IT. Okta is usually disabled at the end of the last working day, but customer-facing transition access may be removed earlier if the separation is sensitive. GitHub, AWS, Salesforce, BambooHR, and Slack access should never remain active after the last working day unless Legal, People Operations, and IT approve a specific exception.

## Process

People Operations provides the written separation packet, release documents if applicable, final payroll timing, and benefits information. Managers handle the role and work transition. Finance confirms payment timing. The employee should receive a clear last working day, last paid day, and point of contact for follow-up questions.

Employees should direct severance questions to People Operations, not to IT or Finance. IT can answer equipment return and access timing questions. Finance can answer payment mechanics after People Operations has finalized the amount. Managers can answer work transition questions, but should not revise the package or speculate about legal terms.

If an employee believes the calculation is wrong, they should reply to the People Operations contact in the separation packet within 10 business days and include the specific issue: start date, completed years, unused vacation balance, local benefit continuation, or payment timing. People Operations will review the record in BambooHR, confirm with Finance if needed, and provide a written response.

Severance is intended to be humane, consistent, and administratively clear. It does not change the confidentiality, invention assignment, security, equipment return, or non-solicitation obligations that continue after employment where those obligations apply.
