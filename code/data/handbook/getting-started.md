# Getting Started

## Before Your First Day

Welcome to NovaOps. We sell workflow automation to mid-market customers, and we work as a remote-first team across Israel, Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States.

People Operations creates your BambooHR profile, sends employment documents, and confirms your start date. IT provisions Okta, Slack, Webex, and your laptop. Customer-facing roles are queued for Salesforce. Engineering roles are queued for Jira, GitHub, and the approved AWS level. If something is missing, open a ticket at [the IT request portal](https://it.novaops.example) rather than asking for a shared password.

Preboarding starts when the signed offer or contractor agreement is complete. By about five business days before your start date, People Operations should have your legal name, preferred name, work location, employment type, and manager. By about three business days before your start date, IT should have the manager-approved access list and shipping address. If your laptop shipment, Okta invite, or calendar invitation is late, tell your manager and file the IT ticket.

Your manager owns your onboarding plan. For example, Maya Cohen, a Customer Success Manager joining in Israel, is assigned a manager in Customer Success, a buddy, a Salesforce training path, and a first-month customer-shadowing schedule before day one. A Backend Engineer such as Daniel Levi should have Jira, GitHub, Slack, approved AWS read-only access, and a first issue or code-reading assignment. A Product Manager should have Jira, Figma, Miro, Slack, and customer notes ready.

Use full names and NovaOps email addresses in onboarding requests. NovaOps has duplicate first names, including Daniel Levi and Daniel Cohen, and has multiple employees named Sara. A ticket that says "please add Daniel to Salesforce" will wait until IT confirms which person you mean.

## Day One

On your first day, sign in through [Okta](https://okta.novaops.example), set up MFA, join Slack, and attend your Webex welcome call. Read the team charter in [the NovaOps wiki](https://wiki.novaops.example), confirm your emergency contact in BambooHR, and check that your name, location, employment type, and manager are correct.

Your manager should give you three things: the outcome your role was hired to create, the first decisions you can make without asking, and the people you should meet in week one. If those are not clear, ask directly.

Day one is not the day to request every tool you have ever used. Start with the systems tied to your role. Sales and Customer Success normally need Salesforce; Customer Success also uses SupportDesk. Engineering normally needs Jira, GitHub, Slack, and, only when justified, AWS or Datadog. Product normally needs Jira, Figma, Miro, Slack, and the wiki. HR and IT access is limited by administrative responsibility.

If Okta works but a downstream app does not, include the app name, error text, and whether you are on a company-managed device. If Salesforce is missing, confirm that your department is Sales, Customer Success, or an approved support role. If GitHub is missing for a contractor, check for explicit manager and IT approval.

If your legal name, preferred name, or email alias is wrong, tell People Operations immediately; name corrections touch payroll, signatures, Okta, Slack display, and customer-facing calendars.

## First Week

Your first week should produce a working setup and a basic map of how your team operates: where work is planned, where decisions are written, how urgent issues escalate, and which recurring meetings matter. NovaOps has a core collaboration window of 13:00-16:00 UTC Tuesday through Thursday.

For a Customer Success Manager, a useful first week might include Salesforce orientation, SupportDesk observation, customer shadowing, and renewal-risk language. For a Backend Engineer, it might include local setup, recent GitHub pull requests, service ownership notes, and a small bug. For a Product Manager, it might include Jira triage, Figma and Miro checks, customer notes, and a decision log review.

Approvals are part of onboarding. Manager approval confirms role need. IT approval confirms access risk, license impact, and least-privilege fit. Finance approval is required for new paid spend above USD 500 per month or USD 2,500 per year, and equipment above USD 1,500 needs Finance review. For unusual requests, include the business reason, system, permission level, and duration.

## First 30 Days

The first month is for context and small wins. Learn how your department uses Slack, Jira, GitHub, Salesforce, BambooHR, Webex, or AWS, depending on your role. Also learn which requests need manager, IT, or Finance approval.

Your manager will schedule a 30-day check-in. Bring notes on what is clear, what is blocked, and which internal systems still feel awkward. People Operations will also check whether your benefits, payroll details, and required compliance acknowledgments are complete.

By day 30, employees should have completed employment documents, payroll setup, emergency contact details, and compliance acknowledgments. Full-time employees usually receive a company-owned laptop, charger, headset, and basic accessories; remote employees may request ordinary remote equipment through [the IT request portal](https://it.novaops.example). Contractors receive only the access and equipment named in the contract or approved by the business owner. Contractor requests for GitHub, production-adjacent data, or customer-facing systems must name the manager sponsor, business purpose, and end date.

Use BambooHR for employee self-service items such as personal details, time away, and HR acknowledgments. Contractor records may use contractor-management workflows instead. Cross-border contractor records need HR review, especially when the contractor is outside the hiring manager's country or the contract terms are changing.

If you transferred teams shortly before joining a new project, verify that your manager and department are current before requesting access. Stale records can send approvals to the wrong person; fix them before making high-risk access requests.

## First 60 to 90 Days

By day 60, you should be doing meaningful work with light supervision. By day 90, you and your manager should agree on normal performance expectations for your level. That does not mean you know everything; it means you know where to look, whom to ask, and how your work is judged. For growth expectations, see [making a career](making-a-career.md).

For most roles, day 60 is a good time to remove temporary scaffolding. If you still depend on a buddy for routine decisions, ask which decisions you now own. If a tool was granted temporarily, confirm whether it should be removed, extended, or converted to regular access. AWS admin, GitHub organization admin, Okta admin, Salesforce admin, and BambooHR administrator rights need a review date or end date.

By day 90, your manager should be able to describe the work you own, the quality bar for your level, and the next few outcomes that matter. You should be able to explain adjacent-team handoffs, role-specific risks, and which systems contain the durable record. Decisions that need history belong in Jira, GitHub, Salesforce, BambooHR, or [the NovaOps wiki](https://wiki.novaops.example), depending on the subject.

If onboarding still feels stuck at day 90, bring your manager a short list: missing access, unclear ownership, confusing rituals, and decisions you cannot make yet.
