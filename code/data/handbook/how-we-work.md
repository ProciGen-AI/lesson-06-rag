# How We Work

## Remote-First, Not Meeting-First

NovaOps is hybrid and remote-first. Some employees work near an office, some are fully remote, and all teams must be able to operate without hallway access. The default is written clarity, thoughtful async updates, and meetings only when conversation will improve the decision.

Our core collaboration window is 13:00-16:00 UTC on Tuesday through Thursday. Teams may set narrower windows when needed, but managers should protect reasonable working hours in each location. If you are in Israel, Germany, the United Kingdom, or the United States, you are not expected to mirror another region's full day. Local holidays, school pickup windows, and daylight-saving changes create real friction; plan around them.

Slack is for short-lived coordination. Jira is for work that needs ownership, priority, or history. GitHub is for code review and engineering decisions. Webex is for live discussion. BambooHR is for HR self-service. Salesforce is for customer and revenue workflows. AWS is used by approved Engineering and IT roles only. For a broader map of systems, see [our internal systems](our-internal-systems.md).

## Async Norms

Async work at NovaOps is not silence. It means the next person has enough context to continue without chasing you. A useful async update includes the current state, next step, owner, decision needed, and any deadline that is real rather than merely preferred. If a task is blocked, name the blocker plainly.

Prefer posting once with the full situation over sending five tiny messages that force another person to reconstruct your thought process. Use threads for side questions, but summarize the outcome in the main channel or in the Jira issue when the answer changes the work. If a Slack discussion produces a decision, copy the conclusion into Jira, GitHub, or the NovaOps wiki at https://wiki.novaops.example before the thread scrolls away.

## Written Decisions

A good NovaOps decision explains the problem, the options considered, the decision, the owner, the date decided, and the review date. Large decisions should be captured in [the NovaOps wiki](https://wiki.novaops.example). Small decisions can live in Jira, GitHub, or Slack if the team can find them later. If a decision affects customers, budget, security, hiring, access, or a release date, write it down.

Decision records should be boring and searchable. Use plain titles such as "Decision: delay EU dashboard beta to 2026-09-15" or "Decision: grant temporary Salesforce export access to Customer Success." Include names, dates, system names, ticket IDs, and the chosen alternative. If the decision later changes, add a dated update instead of editing away the original history.

GitHub pull requests can serve as engineering decision records when tradeoffs are captured in the description or review comments. For roadmap, staffing, and cross-functional work, use Jira or the NovaOps wiki. For HR topics, do not put private employee details in public team spaces.

We do not reward being the fastest person to reply. We reward making work easier for the next person. When you ask for help, include context, the decision you need, the deadline, and what you have already tried.

## Meetings and Live Discussion

Meetings are useful when people need to debate, resolve confusion, or make a decision that would sprawl across twenty messages. They are wasteful when the purpose is status reading.

Every meeting should have an owner, a purpose, and a desired output. The owner sends context in advance, keeps the group on topic, names decisions as they happen, and posts notes afterward. Calendar titles should be specific: "SupportDesk outage retrospective" is better than "follow-up." If the last two recurring sessions had no decisions, cancel or shorten the series.

Default Webex meetings to 25 or 50 minutes. Leave five or ten minutes for people to switch contexts and write notes. If a required decision maker cannot attend, either move the meeting or make the decision async after they can comment.

No one is expected to take calls while driving, during medical appointments, on approved leave, or outside reasonable hours just because a calendar invite exists.

## Response Expectations

For normal internal messages, a same-business-day response is enough. For customer incidents, security issues, payroll blockers, and production incidents, use the team escalation path and the relevant channel. Do not create urgency by tagging many people when one accountable owner is known.

Customer incident exceptions are real exceptions. When a production incident affects a customer, a security issue may expose data, or payroll is blocked before a pay date, teams may interrupt focus time, meet outside the core collaboration window, or ask for a faster handoff. Even then, the accountable lead should name the severity, owner, next update time, and working channel. After the incident, move facts into Jira, SupportDesk, Salesforce, GitHub, or the wiki so the history is not trapped in a live call.

If you need focus time, say so in Slack and block your calendar. If you will be away for more than half a day, update your status and make sure urgent work has a backup owner. Vacations and personal days should be entered in BambooHR. For broader time-off expectations, see [benefits and perks](benefits-and-perks.md).

## Trust and Accountability

Remote work at NovaOps is based on outcomes, not screen watching. Managers should set clear goals and review results. Employees should surface risk early, keep commitments visible, and ask for prioritization help when work exceeds capacity.

When work crosses departments, name one directly responsible person. A project with five interested leaders and no owner is not owned. A project with one owner and many contributors can move.

Accountability is not the same as blame. If a handoff is messy, say what happened and repair the process. If a deadline is slipping, raise it when the risk is visible. Managers should make priorities explicit when work exceeds capacity.

## Disagreement and Conflict

Healthy disagreement is part of the job. NovaOps expects people to challenge ideas, not corner people. Say what you see, what risk is being missed, and what decision you recommend. Useful conflict includes Engineering flagging security risk, Customer Success flagging customer impact, Product protecting the current release, or HR saying a manager's timeline does not leave enough room for fair process.

Do not use public channels to embarrass someone, relitigate a closed decision, or imply bad intent when a simpler explanation fits. If a thread is getting sharp, pause and move to a smaller Webex conversation with a clear owner and note taker. If the conflict involves harassment, discrimination, retaliation, pay, performance management, or private employee information, involve People Ops.

The most useful phrase is often "I disagree because..." followed by a fact, a risk, or a customer impact. If a decision must be made before consensus exists, the accountable owner makes the call, writes down the dissent or tradeoff, and sets a review date.
