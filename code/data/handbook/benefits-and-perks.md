# Benefits and Perks

## Eligibility

NovaOps benefits are designed for a remote-first company with employees in Israel, Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Full-time employees are eligible for the company benefits in their country from their first day unless a local plan document says otherwise. Contractors receive the equipment and access needed for the engagement, but do not receive employee insurance, retirement, or leave benefits.

Questions about your own eligibility should go to People Operations at sara.cohen@novaops.example or through [BambooHR](https://bamboohr.novaops.example). If a local law provides a stronger benefit than this handbook, NovaOps follows the local law. If a signed employment agreement, local plan document, or statutory rule conflicts with this handbook, People Operations confirms which rule controls before payroll or reimbursement is processed.

Common edge cases: interns, agency workers, and contractors are not treated as full-time employees unless People Operations issues a NovaOps employment agreement. Part-time employee benefits are handled in the local plan. If you transfer countries, benefits usually move on the effective payroll transfer date.

## Time Off

NovaOps provides 20 paid vacation and personal days per calendar year for full-time employees, plus 11 local public holidays based on your work location. We do not use an unlimited PTO policy; we want people to plan real rest and for managers to see when teams are running too hot.

Unused vacation rolls over year to year, but your vacation bank is capped at 27 days at any time. Once you reach 27 days, additional accrual pauses until you take time off. Managers should help employees schedule time before they hit the cap. See [state and family leave](stateFMLA.md) for medical, caregiver, and parental leave.

Vacation should be requested in BambooHR before you book travel whenever practical. For one or two days, three business days' notice is usually enough; for a week or more, give at least two weeks' notice. A manager may ask you to shift dates for critical coverage, payroll close, incident response, or a launch, but should also offer a realistic alternate window.

Holiday calendars follow your regular work location. An employee based in Germany receives the Germany public holiday calendar even during a short visit to the United Kingdom. NovaOps does not usually swap public holidays, but managers may approve a vacation or personal day for observances not on your local list.

Q: Do unused vacation days roll over? A: Yes, unused vacation rolls over year to year, but the bank is capped at 27 days and accrual pauses once the cap is reached.

After four continuous years at NovaOps, full-time employees may request a one-time 20 working day paid sabbatical. Sabbaticals require manager and People Operations approval, must be planned at least 90 days ahead, and should not be stacked with resignation notice. A request should include dates, coverage notes, and customer or release risks.

## Health, Family, and Retirement

NovaOps pays the full employee premium for baseline medical coverage where local plans use premiums, and contributes toward dependent coverage according to the country plan. Dental, vision, mental health support, and statutory social benefits are administered locally. Employees may request a benefits explanation through BambooHR.

Baseline medical coverage means the default employee-only plan available in your country. If you choose a richer plan, add dependents, or miss a local enrollment deadline, your payroll deduction may be different. Some countries require local forms before coverage becomes active.

For US employees, the 401(k) retirement plan is with Vanguard. You may enroll with your first paycheck. NovaOps matches 100% of your contribution up to 6% of your salary, subject to the plan rules. Employees outside the US receive the retirement or pension contributions required by their local employment plan.

Q: Does NovaOps match 401(k) contributions? A: For US employees, yes. The plan is with Vanguard, enrollment may begin with your first paycheck, and NovaOps matches 100% of your contribution up to 6% of salary. Employees outside the United States receive the retirement or pension contributions required by their local employment plan.

Primary caregivers welcoming a new child receive up to 16 weeks of leave at 100% pay. Secondary caregivers receive up to 6 weeks at 100% pay. Additional unpaid leave may be available; see [state and family leave](stateFMLA.md). Paid parental leave must be taken within the first year after birth, adoption, surrogacy, or foster placement. Leave may be taken in one block or, with manager and People Operations approval, in two blocks.

Q: How much paid parental leave do I get as the primary caregiver? A: Up to 16 weeks at 100% pay. Secondary caregivers receive up to 6 weeks at 100% pay. Additional unpaid leave may be available depending on local law and approval.

## Work Support and Perks

New employees receive a NovaOps laptop and standard accessories through IT. Remote and hybrid employees may request a home office setup allowance of up to USD 850 in their first year and USD 300 per year after that for ergonomic refreshes. Requests go through [the IT request portal](https://it.novaops.example) and need manager approval; purchases above USD 1,500 also need Finance review by Tom Baker.

Equipment allowances are for work comfort and security: monitor, keyboard, mouse, desk, chair, dock, webcam, headset, lighting, or similar ergonomic tools. They are not for home decor, gaming gear, or personal tablets unless IT approves a role need. Reimbursement normally requires a receipt and proof that the item will be used for NovaOps work. Company-owned equipment is tracked by IT; see [managing work devices](managing-work-devices.md).

Allowances reimburse actual approved spend; they are not cash bonuses. If you spend USD 600, you are reimbursed up to USD 600, not the full USD 850. Currency conversions use the Finance rate in the reimbursement system on the processing date.

Each employee has an annual learning budget of USD 1,500 for books, courses, conferences, or certifications related to their role or likely next role. Managers approve the learning purpose; Finance approves reimbursement. Travel, hotels, and conference tickets count against the same learning budget unless People Operations approves a separate business case.

NovaOps also provides a USD 75 monthly wellbeing allowance, a USD 40 monthly mobile allowance for customer-facing and on-call roles, and a USD 2,500 employee referral bonus for successful full-time hires who remain employed for 90 days. Wellbeing can cover practical health or recovery costs where allowed by local rules, but not alcohol, recreational travel, or family expenses. The mobile allowance is for roles that regularly call customers, respond to incidents, or carry an on-call rotation.

Referral bonuses are paid through payroll after the referred full-time hire completes 90 days of employment and the referring employee is still employed, unless local law requires a different treatment. The referral must be recorded before the candidate reaches final interview. Contractors, interns, recent rehires, and candidates already in an active pipeline are not normally eligible.
